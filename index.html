Total DJs in 2024 once you count the self-employed — about 47% of them. The official wage count misses most of the booth.

From a record bag to a phone: a starter DJ setup fell from roughly $2,000 in the turntable era to about $15 today. Software went free, controllers got cheap, and a streaming subscription replaced the crates. Representative entry-level hardware + software costs assembled from retail prices — illustrative of the trend, not an official index. Music isn’t included; it went from ~$15 a record to a ~$10/mo subscription. Log scale.
Sources — Electronic music revenue: IMS Electronic Music Business Report (MIDiA Research). DJ employment: U.S. BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics (27-2091) and Occupational Outlook Handbook. Unemployment & real GDP: U.S. BLS / FRED (UNRATE, GDPC1). Recession dates: NBER.
Caveats — The DJ workforce is mostly self-employed, so formal counts undercount it. Industry-revenue figures are annual estimates and are occasionally restated between report editions; some years are gaps, not zeros. Real GDP values are approximate — refresh from FRED for exact vintages. Correlation over ~7 shared years is suggestive, not proof.
